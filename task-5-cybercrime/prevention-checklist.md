# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

## 🔐 General Safety Tips (For All Students)

1. **Use strong passwords** – Mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never use the same password for multiple accounts.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** – Add an extra layer of security to your email, social media, and banking apps.

3. **Never share OTP** – Banks, companies, or government agencies will NEVER ask for your OTP. If someone asks, it's a scam.

4. **Verify links before clicking** – Hover over links to see the actual URL. Avoid clicking on suspicious links in messages or emails.

5. **Keep software updated** – Regularly update your phone, laptop, and apps to get the latest security patches.

6. **Use trusted apps only** – Download apps only from official stores (Google Play Store, Apple App Store).

7. **Don't save card details** – Avoid saving credit/debit card details on shopping websites.

8. **Check bank statements regularly** – Monitor your transactions to spot any unauthorized activity early.

## 💰 UPI/Financial Safety Tips (At Least 2 – Mandatory)

9. **Never scan unknown QR codes** – Scammers use fake QR codes to collect money. Only scan QR codes from trusted sources.

10. **Never share UPI PIN** – Your UPI PIN is like your ATM PIN. Never share it with anyone, even if they claim to be from customer support.

11. **Use UPI apps with caution** – Set a small daily transaction limit in your UPI app to minimize potential loss.

12. **Double-check before paying** – Always verify the recipient's name before entering UPI PIN for any payment.

## 🚨 What to Do If You Become a Victim

13. **Report immediately to National Cyber Crime Portal:** www.cybercrime.gov.in

14. **Call Cyber Helpline:** 1930 (24x7 toll-free number)

15. **Call your bank immediately** – Block your account or card to prevent further loss.

16. **Save evidence** – Take screenshots of messages, transaction details, and call logs.
