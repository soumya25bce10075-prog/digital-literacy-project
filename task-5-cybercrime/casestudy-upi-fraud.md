# Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

## What is UPI Fraud?

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where scammers trick victims into sharing sensitive information like UPI PIN, OTP, or scanning fake QR codes to illegally transfer money from their bank accounts.

## How It Typically Happens (Step by Step)

1. **Fake Offer Message:** Victim receives a message on WhatsApp or SMS claiming they have won a prize (e.g., "Congratulations! You won ₹50,000. Click here to claim").

2. **Fake Customer Care Call:** The victim clicks the link and calls the number provided. The scammer pretends to be a customer care executive.

3. **Request for Details:** The scammer asks the victim to share their UPI ID, bank name, and mobile number for verification.

4. **Fake QR Code:** The scammer sends a QR code and says "Scan this to receive money." The victim scans it, but this QR code is actually a "collect money" QR code.

5. **Money Deducted:** When the victim scans and enters their UPI PIN, money is deducted from their account instead of being received.

6. **Blocking:** After the transaction, the scammer blocks the victim's number and disappears.

## Who is Usually Targeted?

- College students who are new to online payments
- Elderly people not familiar with digital payments
- People looking for part-time jobs or easy money offers
- Anyone who is not aware of UPI security practices

## Consequences

- Financial loss – Money is deducted from the victim's account
- Mental stress and panic
- Bank account can be compromised if more details are shared
- Difficulty in recovering money if not reported immediately
- Sometimes victims are tricked into sharing full bank details, leading to further fraud

## Realistic Scenario

A 19-year-old college student received a message saying "Get ₹10,000 cashback on your last purchase. Click to claim." The student clicked the link, called the number, and was asked to scan a QR code to receive the money. After scanning and entering UPI PIN, ₹15,000 was deducted from the account. The scammer blocked the number immediately.

## How to Avoid This

- Never share UPI PIN or OTP with anyone
- Verify any offer or call before trusting
- Only scan QR codes from trusted sources
- Report immediately to cyber helpline if fraud happens
